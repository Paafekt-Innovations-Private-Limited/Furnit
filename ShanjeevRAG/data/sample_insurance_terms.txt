Insurance domain – key terms and concepts

Excess (deductible)
The amount you pay towards a claim before the insurer pays. For example, if your motor excess is £250 and the repair cost is £1,000, you pay £250 and the insurer pays £750. Voluntary excess can lower your premium.

Home insurance – buildings and contents
Buildings cover: the structure, fixtures, and permanent fittings (e.g. pipes, baths). Usually required by mortgage lenders.
Contents cover: belongings inside the home (furniture, electronics, jewellery). Often sold together with buildings.

Motor insurance – UK
Third party: covers damage to others only. Third party, fire and theft: adds fire and theft of your vehicle. Comprehensive: covers your own vehicle and third parties. UK law requires at least third party cover to drive.

Claims process
Notify the insurer as soon as possible. Provide evidence (photos, receipts, police report if relevant). An assessor may be appointed. The insurer may offer a settlement; you can dispute if you believe it is unfair.

Solvency II (EU/UK)
Regulatory framework for insurer capital and risk management. Insurers must hold enough capital to absorb losses and protect policyholders. Reporting and disclosure requirements apply to firms in the EEA and UK.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
Insurance is a data-heavy sector. Personal data (health, claims history, etc.) must be processed lawfully, with clear consent or other legal basis. Data breaches can lead to large fines and must be reported.
