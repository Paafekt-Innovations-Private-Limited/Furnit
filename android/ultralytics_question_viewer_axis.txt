We have an Android WebView 3D room viewer using Three.js and OrbitControls. The room is a Gaussian splatting PLY from a ViT-based model (SHARP, ExecuTorch INT8). The pipeline writes PLY with raw coordinates (x, -y, -z) and computes a bounding box; we pass room dimensions (roomWidth, roomHeight, roomDepth) and bbox center (roomCenterX, roomCenterY, roomCenterZ) to the viewer.

In the viewer we use SparkJS SplatMesh to load the PLY. THREE.Box3.setFromObject(splatMesh) always returns a tiny size (never valid), so we use a fallback path: we set splatMesh.position to (-roomCenterX, -roomCenterY, -roomCenterZ) to center the mesh at origin. We then place the camera at "back wall minus inset" and set controls.target to the room center (0,0,0). For landscape: backWallZ = roomDepth/2, camera at (0, eyeHeight, backWallZ - inset), target (0,0,0). Example from logs: room 8.79 x 7.22 x 4.19 m, center 1.61, 0.90, -3.23, camPos 0.000, -2.008, 0.405.

The problem: the user sees grey initially; when they touch and slide (orbit), the room comes into view. So the room is not in front of the camera at start. The user says "the axis of rotation is wrong."

Questions: (1) Could the model's coordinate system (which way is "front" vs "back", which axis is depth) differ from our assumption, so we are placing the camera in empty space? (2) Should we apply a fixed rotation to the splat mesh for landscape (e.g. 180° around Y) so the "photo view" (interior as seen from the camera position) faces our initial camera? (3) How do we choose the correct "depth" axis and camera/target so the room is visible immediately and orbit rotates around the right axis? We are not using YOLO; this is 3D Gaussian splatting room reconstruction.
