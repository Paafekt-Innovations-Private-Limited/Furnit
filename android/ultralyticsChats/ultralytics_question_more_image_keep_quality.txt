We have a ViT-based 3D reconstruction model (SHARP) on Android with ExecuTorch INT8. The model expects square input (1536x1536). We need to keep 3D quality high while including more of the image (we are losing significant edges with current preprocessing).

**Current preprocessing (quality is good, but we lose edges):**
- We center-crop to a square: side = min(image_width, image_height). So for 2160x3840 we take the center 2160x2160 pixels (crop 840px from left and right). For 3840x2160 we take the center 2160x2160 (crop 840px from left and right).
- We then resize that square to 1536x1536 and feed it to the model.
- We write PLY with raw coordinates (no aspect scaling). Quality is good; no jagged output.
- We tried resize-to-fit + 114-gray letterbox (full image, constant padding) and resize-to-fit + edge-extension—both caused jagged/chaotic 3D output. Ultralytics advised center-crop to match ViT training distribution.

**What we want:**
- Keep the current 3D quality (no jagged geometry).
- Include more of the image so significant edges/content are not discarded. We are willing to accept a slightly different approach if it does not degrade quality.

**Questions:**

1. Is there a cropping strategy that uses more of the image than a strict center square (min x min) while still matching what the ViT expects and avoiding jagged output? For example: a larger square that overlaps more of the long dimension (with some content outside the crop), or multiple crops merged later?

2. Could we use a slightly larger input square (e.g. crop to 1.1 * min(w,h) and then resize down to 1536, or crop a rectangle and resize to 1536x1536 with minimal stretch) without breaking the model—or would that definitely hurt quality?

3. If center-crop to square is the only safe option for this class of model, is there a best practice for choosing *which* square (e.g. bias the crop toward the top or one side based on content) so we lose less important content, while still feeding a single square?

4. Any other deployment-friendly way to get “more image” into the pipeline without letterbox/edge-pad (which caused artifacts) and without degrading 3D quality?

We are not using YOLO; this is SHARP 3D Gaussian splatting from a single photo.
